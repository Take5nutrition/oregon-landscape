Damascus's larger lots and more rural character make it one of the best places in the Portland metro for truly naturalistic water features. There's room here to build something that feels like it's always been part of the land — a boulder waterfall, a recirculating stream through a meadow garden, a pond with natural planting edges.

Oregon Landscape designs water features that fit the Damascus setting: organic, durable, and scaled to the property. We have the heavy equipment to work on larger sites and the design sensibility to make every feature look completely at home.

Common Questions

Water Features in Damascus — FAQ

Can Damascus's large lots accommodate a naturalistic pond or stream?

Damascus properties are among the best-suited in the metro for naturalistic water features — large acreage, rural character, and less regulatory complexity mean you can build a genuine pond, stream system, or even a swimming pond at a scale that urban lots can't accommodate.

Does a Damascus septic system limit where we can put a water feature?

Yes — water features need to be sited away from septic drain fields and any county-required setbacks. We always request as-built records before design so features are placed where they won't interfere with your septic system now or in the future.

What's a realistic maintenance schedule for a Damascus pond?

Ponds in rural settings with more organic input — leaves, wildlife, natural runoff — need attentive maintenance, especially in fall. We design robust filtration systems appropriate for the site conditions and offer seasonal service to keep the system healthy.

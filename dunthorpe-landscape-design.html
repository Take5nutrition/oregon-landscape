Common Questions

Landscape Design in Dunthorpe — FAQ

What makes Dunthorpe different from other Portland-area markets?

Dunthorpe is unincorporated Multnomah County with very large parcels, significant mature tree canopy, and properties that often need estate-scale landscape planning — not just a patio and some plants. We're accustomed to working at that scope and pace.

Are there HOA or deed restrictions in Dunthorpe?

Dunthorpe doesn't have a traditional HOA, but as unincorporated Multnomah County, county land use rules apply. Some streets also have private deed restrictions affecting landscaping changes. We review these before design begins so there are no compliance issues later.

What are typical project sizes in Dunthorpe?

Projects here often involve multiple acres, long existing driveways, significant mature trees, and integration with historic landscape elements. We bring the same careful planning and high-end material standards to estate-scale projects that we bring to any residential landscape.

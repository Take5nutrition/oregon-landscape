Common Questions

Water Features in Hillsboro — FAQ

Does the Washington County area have any water feature permitting requirements?

For most residential water features in Hillsboro — ponds, pondless systems, fountains — permits aren't typically required. Larger excavations and any features affecting drainage patterns may need review. We check applicable rules before design.

What's the typical soil condition for water feature installation in Hillsboro?

Hillsboro's newer developments often have compacted fill soil with variable drainage. We assess subgrade conditions before excavating to ensure liner and filtration systems are installed on a stable, well-drained base.

How do Hillsboro's water features hold up through summer drought?

The Willamette Valley's dry summers do cause evaporation — a properly sized pond with auto-fill typically loses 1–2 inches per week in summer. We size auto-fill valves appropriately and specify water-efficient recirculating systems.

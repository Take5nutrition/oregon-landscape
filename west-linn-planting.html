Plants Selected for the Pacific Northwest

West Linn's wooded character and mature tree canopy create specific planting conditions — shade-tolerant ground covers, understory shrubs, and plants selected for woodland settings rather than open sun. We design planting plans that work with the environment, not against it.

We also handle full-sun planting for open areas, privacy screening installations, and ornamental planting for patios and entry areas. Whatever the conditions on your property, we match the plant palette to the site.

We handle all phases of planting work: clearing and bed preparation, soil amendment, installation, mulching, and initial watering. We also connect planting plans to irrigation design when applicable.

Common Questions

Planting in West Linn — FAQ

What plants handle West Linn's shaded, wooded lots best?

Many West Linn properties have significant shade from mature Douglas fir and big-leaf maple canopy. We select shade-tolerant species — native ferns, rhododendrons, Oregon grape, hellebores, and hostas — that thrive in low-light conditions rather than fighting the site's natural character.

Is deer pressure significant in West Linn?

Deer pressure in West Linn is moderate to high, particularly on properties bordering natural areas and the Tualatin foothills. We incorporate deer-resistant species as the backbone of planting designs and can advise on physical deterrents for high-value specimens.

What planting strategies help stabilize West Linn's slopes?

Slope stabilization planting focuses on deep-rooting plants with dense surface coverage — native ferns, salal, kinnikinnick for ground cover, and woody shrubs like Oregon grape and red-flowering currant. We combine plant selection with erosion control measures during the establishment period.

Landscape Planting

Plants Selected for the Pacific Northwest

Great planting design is about more than aesthetics — it's about choosing the right species for your soil, sun exposure, and Oregon's specific climate conditions. Plants that thrive here, in the right spots, look better and require far less intervention over time.

Oregon Landscape designs planting plans that deliver four-season interest — spring color, summer texture, fall foliage, and winter structure. Every plant selection is informed by 16+ years of working in Portland's wet winters and dry summers.

We handle all phases of planting work: clearing and bed preparation, soil amendment, installation, mulching, and initial watering. We also connect our planting plans to your irrigation design when applicable.

What We Plant

Planting Categories

Shade & Ornamental Trees

Canopy trees for privacy and shade, plus ornamental specimens for seasonal color and year-round structure. Properly staked and root-zone mulched at installation.

Screens & Hedges

Privacy screening with evergreen shrubs and hedgerows — arborvitae, laurel, boxwood, and more — sized and spaced for quick coverage with minimal maintenance.

Perennials & Groundcovers

Hardy perennials, native groundcovers, and seasonal color plants that fill in beds, suppress weeds, and bring the landscape to life from spring through fall.

Ornamental Grasses

Fountaingrass, Karl Foerster, and other ornamental grasses add texture and movement to the landscape — low-maintenance and striking in every season.

Seasonal Color

Annual and perennial seasonal color plantings for beds, containers, and entryways — refreshed for spring and fall to keep your landscape looking its best year-round.

Bed Preparation & Mulching

Every planting job includes proper bed prep — weed removal, soil amendment, and a clean layer of bark mulch to conserve moisture and discourage weeds.

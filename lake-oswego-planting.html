Common Questions

Planting in Lake Oswego — FAQ

Does Lake Oswego's tree ordinance affect what we can remove before planting?

Yes — before we can remove any trees to make room for new planting areas, we check caliper size against LO's protected tree thresholds. We design around protected trees where possible, and manage the removal permit process when a tree does need to come out.

What ornamental plant standards are expected in Lake Oswego?

Lake Oswego clients typically expect multi-layered planting with premium ornamentals — Japanese maples, specimen conifers, blooming shrubs with long seasons of interest, and perennial borders with four-season appeal. We design to that standard.

How do we manage planting near the lake shoreline?

Shoreline planting in Lake Oswego needs to work within applicable buffer requirements while stabilizing the bank and providing natural habitat. Native riparian species — red-twig dogwood, native willow, iris — are both ecologically appropriate and visually appealing in these areas.

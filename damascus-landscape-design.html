Common Questions

Landscape Design in Damascus — FAQ

Is Damascus rural, suburban, or something in between?

Damascus occupies a unique position — it was incorporated, then disincorporated, and is now governed by Clackamas County as unincorporated land. Most properties are large (1–5+ acres) with a rural-residential character, and landscaping needs to fit that scale.

Do septic systems affect landscape design on Damascus properties?

Many Damascus properties are on septic systems with drain fields, which creates real constraints on tree placement and hardscape. We always request as-built septic records and design around drain field locations so nothing has to be redone later.

Are permits simpler in unincorporated Clackamas County?

Generally yes — Clackamas County tends to have less regulatory complexity than incorporated cities for most residential landscaping. Grading over certain volumes and structures over a certain size still require permits, but the review process is typically more direct.

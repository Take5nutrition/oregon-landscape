Common Questions

Landscape Design in Clackamas — FAQ

How does Clackamas County permitting compare to city permitting?

Clackamas County is generally more straightforward for residential landscaping than incorporated cities like Portland or Lake Oswego. Retaining walls over 4 feet and structures still require permits, but the process tends to move faster. As a Clackamas-based company, we know the local process well.

What soil conditions are common in Clackamas?

Clackamas soils tend to be clay-heavy, which creates drainage challenges if not addressed properly. We incorporate French drains, amended planting beds, and proper subgrade grading as standard practice on most projects here.

Are there HOA restrictions we need to plan around?

Some newer Clackamas-area neighborhoods have HOA landscape standards, but many don't. We review any HOA documentation before design begins so compliance is built in from the start — not retrofitted after the fact.

Hardscape That Works As Hard As It Looks

Milwaukie's mix of bungalow lots and larger suburban properties calls for hardscape solutions that work across a range of footprints. We design patios and walkways that make the most of the space available — whether that's a compact flagstone sitting area or a full paver patio with seating walls.

Every project includes proper base prep, drainage planning, and clean edge detailing. Our Milwaukie clients consistently note that the finished hardscape makes the whole yard feel more intentional and complete.

We work with flagstone, concrete pavers, slate, and bluestone, and we treat every sub-base as seriously as the surface. The part no one sees is what determines how your patio looks in ten years.

Hardscape Materials We Install

Flagstone & Natural Stone

Irregular flagstone creates a natural, organic look that ages beautifully. Available in a range of colors and textures to complement any home style.

Concrete Pavers

Manufactured concrete pavers offer a clean, uniform look and are available in dozens of shapes, sizes, and colors — including tumbled styles for a more natural feel.

Slate & Bluestone

Cut slate and bluestone provide a refined, formal appearance — well-suited to contemporary homes and modern landscape designs.

Steps & Staircases

Natural stone steps and staircases integrated into the landscape — built level, properly pitched for drainage, and designed to flow naturally with your terrain.

Common Questions

Patios & Walkways in Milwaukie — FAQ

How do we maximize a patio space on Milwaukie's urban lots?

On smaller Milwaukie lots, every square foot of patio needs to earn its place. We design with defined zones — dining, seating, planted edges — so the patio feels organized and complete rather than a featureless slab with furniture.

Does Milwaukie have stormwater requirements for new hardscape?

Milwaukie's stormwater program may apply to larger impervious surface additions. We assess your lot coverage and design drainage appropriately, including permeable sections where stormwater rules are a factor.

What walkway materials work well in Milwaukie's urban infill context?

Milwaukie's urban neighborhoods often suit concrete pavers or natural stone that bridge the gap between the street and the home with a quality material that reads as intentional and elevated — not the poured concrete typically used for builder walks.

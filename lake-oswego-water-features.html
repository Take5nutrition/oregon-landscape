Common Questions

Water Features in Lake Oswego — FAQ

Does Lake Oswego's tree ordinance affect pond placement?

Yes — protected trees have root zones that limit nearby excavation. We map tree protection zones before siting any water feature so construction doesn't damage tree roots or require costly variance permits.

What water feature styles fit Lake Oswego's high-end aesthetic?

Lake Oswego clients typically favor natural stone waterfalls, formal reflecting pools, and large-scale koi ponds with premium filtration. We use quality materials — real boulders, professional-grade equipment — that match the standard of the homes we work on.

Does a water feature near the lake require special permits?

Lakefront properties have shoreline setback requirements and surface water runoff concerns. Water features near the lake need to be designed so they don't contribute to runoff or alter the shoreline, and permits may apply. We review this before design.
